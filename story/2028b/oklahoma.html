Oklahoma, USA – April

Emily Carter got a call from the bank. They weren’t renewing her farm loan.

“This isn’t personal,” the woman on the line said. “But with the market conditions—”

Emily hung up. She already knew.

No wheat meant no money. No money meant no farm. No farm meant nothing.

She looked out at the fields again, at the dry, broken stalks. She had never seen his land look so empty.
